Shaping the Future of Education Together

April 26–29, 2022, in Lisbon, Portugal

Leading experts in education technology will discuss the latest research, new approaches to learning, and best practices for integrating new technologies into the virtual classroom.

This conference will be centered around expanding access to education globally, the future of work, and using open technology to solve today's challenges of up-skilling, re-skilling, and delivering life-long learning.

Conference attendees will learn about innovative uses of technology in higher education, inside corporations, and across nations. Attendees will also learn about advancements in instructional design, course production, and methods for operating, extending, and enhancing the Open edX platform.

  • Tuesday: long-form tutorials and workshops
  • Wednesday/Thursday: 45-minute breakouts and 10-minute lightning talks
  • Friday: in-depth summits for developers and instructional designers
  • Registration includes light breakfast, lunch, coffee breaks, and a cocktail reception on Wednesday.

If you are interested in sponsorship options, please contact the Open edX Community Manager, Eden Huthmacher, at ehuthmacher@tcril.org.

Registration

Q: Can I register in person in the event?

A: Registration for the 2022 Open edX Conference is online only, and registration closes on April 18th, 2022. 

Q: What is included in the registration fee?

A: All tickets include:

  • Four full days of the conference
  • Conference program
  • Breakfast, lunch, and coffee/tea break each day
  • Cocktail reception on Wednesday, 4/27/22.

Q: Which ticket categories are available? 

A:

  • Student Rate 
  • Early-Bird Non-Profit Rate (valid through 2/28/22)
  • Regular Non-Profit Rate
  • Early-Bird Corporate Rate (valid through 2/28/22) 
  • Regular Corporate Rate

Q: What types of payments do you accept for the conference fee?

A: All payments are processed through the Eventbrite payment processing system and accept major credit cards, such as Visa, Mastercaord, American Express, and Discover 

Q.: What is the registration cancellation policy?

A.: All ticket sales are final. If you purchase a ticket for the conference and later decide not to attend, the event organizer will not issue a refund. There are no refunds, exchanges, or credits for any purchases unless the event organizer decides to cancel the conference.

Q.: I have dietary restrictions, can you accommodate them?

Covid-19

Q.: Can I travel to Lisbon, Portugal if I’m not vaccinated or have not obtained any of the approved vaccines, as outlined by the Portuguese National Health Services?

A.: Yes, unvaccinated travelers are welcome. There are several testing options for eligible non-vaccinated passengers arriving in Mainland Portugal and the Azores: 

OPTION 1 Negative NAAT test taken 72 hours before departure 

  • Test type accepted: nucleic acid amplification test (NAAT) 
  • Time window: 72 hours before departure from the first embarkation point. 
  • Applicable age: Travelers 12 years and older. 
  • Keep in mind: 
    • Self-tests are not accepted. 
    • Passengers could be subject to PCR test upon arrival 
    • Passengers may present an EU Digital COVID (negative test) certificate as proof of a negative COVID-19 test result. 

OPTION 2 Negative Rapid Antigen test taken 24 hours before departure 

  • Test type accepted: Rapid antigen test (RAT) 
  • Time window: 24 hours before departure from the first embarkation point. 
  • Applicable age: Travelers 12 years and older. 
  • Keep in mind: 
    • Self-tests are not accepted. 
    • Passengers could be subject to PCR test upon arrival. 
    • Passengers may present an EU Digital COVID (negative test) certificate as proof of a negative COVID-19 test result.

OPTION 3 EU Digital COVID (Recovery) Certificate/Recovery certificate 

  • Certificates accepted: 
    • EU Digital COVID (Recovery) Certificate. 
    • Recovery certificates issued in Albania, Andorra, Armenia, Faroe Isl., Georgia, Iceland, Israel, Liechtenstein, Moldova, Monaco, Morocco, New Zealand, North Macedonia (Rep.), Norway, Panama, San Marino, Serbia, Singapore, Switzerland, Togo, Turkey, Ukraine, the United Kingdom or Vatican City (Holy See). 
  • Time window: At least 11 and not more than 180 days before arrival. 
  • Applicable age: Travelers 12 years and older. 
  • Keep in mind: 
    • Passengers could be subject to PCR test upon arrival. 
    • Recovery certificates must include the following information: 
      • Name, surname and birthdate of the holder 
      • Disease or agent from which the holder recovered: COVID-19 
      • Date of first positive diagnostic test result 
      • The member state or third country in which the diagnostic test was performed 
      • Certificate issuing entity 

Source of information: Portuguese National Health Services and the Portugal Ministry of Tourism.

Event Details

Q.: Where can I find conference schedules?

A.: Detailed schedules will be posted on the 2022 Open edX Conference website closer to the event. Here is an overview of what you can expect:

  • Day 1 (Tuesday): Long-form tutorials and workshops. 
  • Day 2 (Wednesday): Keynotes, 45-minute breakouts and 10-minute lightning talks. Cocktail reception.
  • Day 3 (Thursday): Keynotes, 45-minute breakouts and 10-minute lightning talks
  • Day 4 (Friday): in-depth summits for developers and instructional designers

Note: You can also check out the Open edX Conference 2019 schedule from the last Open edX in-person conference in 2019, to get a rough idea about topics and conference structure.

Q.: I have never been to an Open edX Conference, will it be useful for me?

A.: New participants are very welcome! You can watch previously recorded conference presentations on the Open edX YouTube channel to see if the format and content suits your needs. The 2022 Open edX Conference will provide tremendous insight to attendees about in depth technical and functional capabilities of the Open edX platform, in order for attendees to evaluate whether to use the Open edX platform for their own projects and/or help place the Open edX platform on the map of available e-learning technologies.

Q.: Will there be Wi-Fi?

A.: Free Wi-Fi will be available at the conference site. Access information will be printed in the program that you will get when you check in on the first day.

Q.: What about transportation during the conference?

A.: All conference events will be held at the Nova SBE campus, which is fully accessible, and the reception will be at a restaurant that is a 10-minute walk from campus. If you have a mobility issue that prevents the walk, please include this on your registration form, and we will arrange transportation.

Note: You will need to provide your own transportation from your accommodation to the conference site and back. There are public transportation options and Uber is widely used in Lisbon. We will post transportation options on our website in the coming weeks.

Q.: Any hotel accommodation tips?

A.: Please secure your own accommodation. While there is no particular conference hotel this year, we recommend the following:

  • Sofitel Lisbon Liberdade
  • Sheraton Lisboa Hotel and Spa

Note: April is a high tourist season in Portugal — hotel rooms will get booked quickly. Please make reservations soon to assure your accommodations.

 

Visa Requirements

Q.: Do I need a visa to go to Portugal?

A.: You might, depending on your country of citizenship. Citizens of certain countries require a visa to enter Portugal. Please contact your local Portuguese consulate for guidance on all current and applicable visa requirements.

Note: Visa processing times vary and may be long. Please start your visa process as soon as possible.

Q.: Will you provide visa support for me?

A.: You will need to take care of your own visa application. However, we are happy to provide you with a letter of invitation in support of your visa application. Please contact ehuthmacher@tcril.org to request one.
